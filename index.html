Munnar - Hill Station Paradise

About Munnar

Munnar is one of the most popular hill stations in Kerala and in South India. Munnar is located around 1,600 meters above sea level, in the Western Ghats mountain range. Munnar was the favoured summer resort of the erstwhile British rulers in colonial days.

Key Attractions

  • Tea Gardens: Endless rolling hills covered with tea plantations
  • Eravikulam National Park: Home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr
  • Mattupetty Dam: Beautiful reservoir surrounded by hills
  • Top Station: Highest point offering panoramic views
  • Tea Museum: Learn about tea processing and history

Best Time to Visit

September to March is the ideal time to visit Munnar when the weather is pleasant and perfect for sightseeing.

Activities

Trekking, tea plantation visits, boating, wildlife spotting, photography, and nature walks.

Alleppey - Venice of the East

About Alleppey (Alappuzha)

Alleppey is a city on the Laccadive Sea in southern India's Kerala state. It's the hub of Kerala's backwaters network, a labyrinthine system of canals, rivers, lakes and inlets dotted with traditional houseboats.

Key Attractions

  • Backwaters: Network of canals, rivers, and lakes
  • Houseboats: Traditional Kettuvallams converted to floating hotels
  • Alleppey Beach: Beautiful coastline with lighthouse
  • Vembanad Lake: Largest lake in Kerala
  • Snake Boat Races: Traditional boat racing during festivals

Best Time to Visit

November to February offers the most pleasant weather for backwater cruises and outdoor activities.

Activities

Houseboat cruising, fishing, bird watching, village visits, coir making demonstrations, and beach activities.

Kochi - Queen of Arabian Sea

About Kochi (Cochin)

Kochi is a major port city on the southwest coast of India. It's known for its eclectic architecture, from Portuguese colonial structures to Dutch palaces and British-era buildings.

Key Attractions

  • Fort Kochi: Historic area with colonial architecture
  • Chinese Fishing Nets: Iconic fishing nets at sunset
  • Mattancherry Palace: Portuguese palace with Kerala murals
  • Jewish Synagogue: Oldest active synagogue in Commonwealth
  • Spice Markets: Aromatic spice trading centers

Best Time to Visit

October to March provides comfortable weather for exploring the historic sites and waterfront areas.

Activities

Heritage walks, spice shopping, boat rides, cultural performances, art gallery visits, and street food tours.

Thekkady - Wildlife Paradise

About Thekkady

Thekkady is a popular tourist destination located in the Idukki district of Kerala. It's famous for the Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary and its artificial lake formed by the Mullaperiyar Dam.

Key Attractions

  • Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary: Home to elephants, tigers, and diverse wildlife
  • Periyar Lake: Scenic lake formed by Mullaperiyar Dam
  • Spice Plantations: Cardamom, pepper, and other spice gardens
  • Bamboo Rafting: Adventure activity on Periyar Lake
  • Elephant Junction: Elephant rides and interactions

Best Time to Visit

September to April is ideal for wildlife viewing and outdoor activities in comfortable weather.

Activities

Wildlife safaris, boat cruises, spice plantation tours, trekking, bamboo rafting, and elephant interactions.

Kovalam - Beach Paradise

About Kovalam

Kovalam is an internationally renowned beach destination in Kerala. The word Kovalam means 'grove of coconut trees' and true to its name, the place is abundant with coconut tree groves.

Key Attractions

  • Lighthouse Beach: Most popular beach with iconic lighthouse
  • Hawah Beach: Peaceful crescent-shaped beach
  • Samudra Beach: Less crowded beach perfect for relaxation
  • Vizhinjam: Historic fishing village nearby
  • Ayurvedic Centers: Traditional healing and wellness centers

Best Time to Visit

December to March offers perfect beach weather with calm seas and pleasant temperatures.

Activities

Sunbathing, swimming, surfing, Ayurvedic treatments, yoga, fishing, and beach volleyball.

Wayanad - Green Paradise

About Wayanad

Wayanad is a rural district in Kerala state, southwest India. In the east, the Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary is a lush, forested region with areas of high altitude, home to animals including Asiatic elephants, tigers, leopards and egrets.

Key Attractions

  • Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary: Rich biodiversity and wildlife
  • Chembra Peak: Highest peak in Wayanad with heart-shaped lake
  • Banasura Sagar Dam: Largest earthen dam in India
  • Edakkal Caves: Prehistoric cave paintings
  • Soochipara Falls: Three-tiered waterfall perfect for swimming

Best Time to Visit

October to May is the best time to explore Wayanad's natural attractions and outdoor activities.

Activities

Trekking, wildlife safaris, cave exploration, waterfall visits, plantation tours, and adventure sports.
